Anabelle Smith and Melissa Tapper among Commonwealth Games athletes ruing axed sports
Fri 24 Jul 2026 at 10:07pm
As the Commonwealth Games kicked off on its first full day of competition, plenty more sat at at home, many wishing it could be them.
Organisers in 2026 ditched badminton, hockey, wrestling, road cycling, mountain biking, rhythmic gymnastics, rugby sevens, squash, table tennis, triathlon, beach volleyball, cricket and, the most storied of all, diving.
It has been held at every edition since the first Empire Games in 1930, until this year.
Of the 22 Commonwealth Games diving events, Anabelle Smith went to the past four, including Glasgow 2014.
Twelve years ago she won bronze in the women's synchronised 3m springboard with Maddison Keeney, but not in Glasgow like the rest of her team.
The Scottish capital did not have a facility to allow for diving, so Smith, Keeney and Co were shipped to Edinburgh, the site of the 1970 and 1986 Games.
"[There was] obvious disappointment from the divers … but also an understanding," Smith told ABC Sport.
"It made sense they weren't just gonna ship the divers up to Edinburgh, disappointing but they'll power on and look ahead now."
The 33-year-old had not been planning to defend her 3m synchro gold medal from Birmingham 2022, but is instead attending her fifth Games as a spectator and part of the Australian team's connection and belonging squad.
She's joined by other athletes who have seen their sports axed, including beach volleyballer Chris McHugh and Melissa 'Milly' Tapper.
Her sport of table tennis was also axed after being included in six straight Games from 2002 to 2022.
Tapper, an Olympian and Paralympian, embodies the inclusive and integrated nature of the Games perhaps better than anyone.
She won team bronze at Glasgow 2014 and singles gold on home soil on the Gold Coast in 2018, and is still getting the chance to flash her skills in the team lounge at the decked-out Glasgow Argyle Hotel.
"I've got a little bit of a call-up having the table tennis table in there," she said.
"We've had plenty of athletes and even some staff come through and challenge. Being around athletes everyone's competitive. It's a lot of fun.
"So even though table tennis isn't technically in the Games, it's still a part of the Games."
Table tennis was a "core sport" for the Commonwealth Games and falls into the category of many of the 2026 events, in that it could be held in existing halls.
Field hockey, however, requires a bit more space and bespoke facilities. Its history in the Commonwealth Games is not quite as long as diving's, but it had an elevated status as a "core sport".
In 2014, it was held at the Glasgow National Hockey Centre, which remains operational and is located along the River Clyde, within the 13-kilometre corridor of the pared-back 2026 Games.
When cuts were being made for 2026, the International Hockey Federation (FIH) and Commonwealth member nations lobbied for the sport's inclusion, but they missed out as organisers opted largely for indoor sports with athletics the only outdoor event.
The Kookaburras have won all seven men's gold medals since 1998, while the Hockeyroos have also never missed the podium, boasting four gold, two silver and a bronze.
Kookaburras captain Eddie Ockenden carried the flag in the Birmingham 2022 opening ceremony, as evidence of the sport's standing in Australia's Commonwealth Games movement, so Hockey Australia was "naturally disappointed" to miss out in 2026.
"Our national teams only play a finite number of international tournaments, making every opportunity to compete in one incredibly valuable," high performance director Bernard Savage said.
"The Commonwealth Games have always been one of those opportunities that our teams have genuinely looked forward to. Beyond the competition itself, the Commonwealth Games provide a unique multi-sport environment that our athletes look forward to and aspire to be a part of.
"The broader interest in the Games has provided an opportunity for our athletes and teams to inspire the next generation of hockey players across Australia and the Commonwealth.
"While this has been disappointing news, our focus remains firmly on our preparation for the 2026 FIH Hockey World Cup."
The World Cup will be played in Belgium and The Netherlands from August 15-30, shortly after the end of the Glasgow Games.

Gail Porter reveals grisly injuries after being ‘knocked over’ in London
‘Don’t ever be disrespectful to anyone for how they look or how they are,’ the presenter wrote as she showed bruises
- Bookmark
- CommentsGo to comments
Gail Porter has revealed she sustained cuts and bruises after being knocked over at a London tube station – while condemning “keyboard warriors” who’d criticised her appearance.
The Scottish television presenter and stand-up comedian posted photos of her injuries to her Instagram, explaining that she had a “wee accident” the week before attending the press night of Trainspotting the Musical, where she appeared on the red carpet with covered-up bruises.
It was unclear where Porter had seen abuse about her appearance, but she wrote in a social media post: “To all you wonderful keyboard warriors that said I’m looking old, and saying something was wrong with my face… I went to Trainspotting the Musical to support my friends that created this. Last week I was knocked over while going down the stairs at a London Tube Station. It was just a wee accident on the stairs. Nothing intentional!”
She continued: “My scars are superficial and it’s not a problem. It was an accident and it won’t stop me being me. Please… from the bottom of my heart... don’t ever be disrespectful to anyone for how they look or how they are. Kindness is so invaluable.”
Porter then shared a selfie taken on the night of the Trainspotting event, writing: “I’m a wee Scottish Warrior. Just always look out for each other. Simple x. And good concealer!!!”
In a third post, she captioned a selfie from the same night with: “A few bruises well covered up! The wonder of make up!!!!”
Many fans and supporters offered their sympathies to Porter in the comments section of her posts, with one person writing: “The toxicity out there is overwhelming at times.” The band Mystery Jets commented: “Sending healing vibes Gail, get better soon.”
Porter was shot to fame in the Nineties, presenting shows including Top of the Pops and gracing the covers of numerous men’s magazines of the era. Notoriously a nude photograph of Porter was projected onto the House of Commons in 1999 by FHM Magazine, a stunt Porter wasn’t told about before it happened.
“I got scared of going out because lots of lads stopped me in the street going, ‘Weyheyhey!’” she told The Guardian in 2024. “I tried not to go out at night because of drunk people coming up to you going: ‘I’ve seen your arse.’ It was quite lechy.”
The 55-year-old has been open about her struggles in the years since, too, which have included mental illness and periods of homelessness. In 2004 she was diagnosed with the condition alopecia, after her hair began to fall out.
She has since become a stand-up comic while continuing to present and write. Porter is performing at the Edinburgh Fringe in August, with her stand-up show Gail Porter: Portergeist. A description of the show reads: “She’s been famous, she’s been homeless, she’s been hit-on by the randy ghost of Jim Morrison … When you’ve been sectioned with two blokes who both claim to be Jesus, it’s easy to be cynical – but Gail reminds us why life is worth living (even when you’re dead).”

House Democrat wants Barron Trump to testify about ‘political interference’ in Andrew and Tristan Tate extradition
Rep. Yassamin Ansari is seeking government records and communications surrounding the manospher influencers facing sexual abuse and trafficking allegations
- Bookmark
Democratic Rep. Yassamin Ansari wants Donald Trump’s son Barron Trump to testify to the House Oversight Committee about whether he knew about or “had any involvement whatsoever” in the crimes of Andrew and Tristan Tate, who are accused of strangling women unconscious and raping them.
Federal law enforcement agents arrested the manosphere influencers in Miami on Saturday following an extradition request from the U.K., where they face serious trafficking and sexual abuse allegations.
The brothers, who maintained ties to powerful figures in the administration’s orbit, are not expected to get any relief from the White House, which will not block their extradition, according to press secretary Karoline Leavitt.
But Barron Trump was reportedly a fan of Andrew Tate and spoke to him over Zoom in 2024, according to The New York Times. Donald Trump Jr. also reportedly met Tate at Trump Tower in 2017, and Alina Habba, a former attorney to the president who briefly served as the top federal prosecutor in New Jersey, described herself as a “big fan” during a January 2025 podcast appearance.
Ansari has urged Republican Rep. James Comer, the chair of the powerful House committee, to “swiftly open an investigation into the Tate brothers’ coordination and extensive relationships with members of the president’s orbit, including his son, Barron Trump.”
Two days before their arrests, the brothers were guests of Paolo Zampolli, who serves in the Trump administration as a special envoy for global partnerships, at a party in Washington, D.C.
Paul Ingrassia — the acting general counsel of the General Services Administration who previously served on the Tates’ legal team — also criticized their arrests as “politically driven prosecutions.”
But Trump’s allies appear to have distanced themselves from the Tates in the wake of their arrests.
“Recent reporting and public disclosures raise extremely grave national security, diplomatic, and ethical concerns about whether political networks in the president's orbit, including members of his family, and other public officials leveraged their influence to shield these men,” Ansari wrote to Comer on Thursday.
Ansari’s letter also follows a statement from a Republican colleague who came to the brothers’ defense after the charges were announced.
Rep. Abe Hamadeh called the charges against them “unclear and political.”
“No American court has charged them with a crime despite years of false alarms and innuendos,” Hamadeh wrote on X on July 20. “The US government should either charge them with a crime or protect them from reckless court proceedings in the UK and Romania.”
He said the brothers are “American citizens and so they deserve full American Constitutional protections — which means innocent until proven guilty.”
In her letter, Ansari urged the committee to “investigate whether back-channel communications between the Tate brothers, presidential advisors, and members of the First Family such as Barron Trump influenced official actions or administration policy regarding criminal prosecution and investigation of the brothers or international law enforcement cooperation.”
She also is requesting records from the Department of Justice and the State Department, including “all communications, cables, internal memoranda, notes, and logs of contact” between U.S. and British or Romanian authorities.
Ansari is also seeking “all records of communications” between the White House and federal agencies “regarding the Tate brothers, including, but not limited to, investigation into the Tate brothers’ crimes, potential prosecution of the Tate brothers, political or legal favors given to the Tate brothers by members of the administration such as slow-walking criminal investigations and failing to detain the brothers upon entry into the United States, and the status of extradition requests involving the Tate brothers.”
The committee “must learn from Barron Trump about his knowledge of the Tate brothers' crimes, if he had any involvement whatsoever, and if he communicated with members of his father's administration or the president himself regarding their legal proceedings,” she wrote.
The Independent has requested comment from the White House.
The Tates were banned from traveling outside Romania in late 2022 after their arrests on allegations of trafficking and money laundering.
They have been under a Department of Homeland Security investigation since 2023, and the state of Florida opened a criminal investigation into the brothers last year.
On Saturday, U.K. prosecutors unveiled 38 additional charges against the brothers in addition to 21 counts stemming from previous allegations.
The new charges against Andrew Tate include three counts of arranging or facilitating trafficking for sexual exploitation, three counts of assault occasioning actual bodily harm and 19 additional charges stemming from “indecent images of a child and extreme pornography.”
Tristan Tate is also facing one count of sexual assault, two counts of rape and three counts of arranging or facilitating trafficking for sexual exploitation.
After U.S. Marshals arrested them in Miami, their attorney Joseph D. McBride called the allegations “madness” without a “shred of evidence.”
He told The Independent that the brothers are victims of “political persecution” in the U.K. and characterized the charges as an attempt to “disrupt the judicial process” in the U.S.
The Tates are expected to remain behind bars in Florida while a federal judge determines whether British authorities have met a legal threshold for extradition, which would be approved by the State Department.
Asked about the extradition request this week, Secretary of State Marco Rubio said “there’s no role for us to play at this moment or maybe ever, but I’m not going to opine on that.”
