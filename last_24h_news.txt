Jill Biden was caught off guard by Kamala Harris’ remark about Joe Biden in her memoir
Jill Biden has admitted that she was surprised by a statement former Vice President Kamala Harris made about former President Joe Biden in her memoir.
Harris wrote in her book that Joe Biden’s ego and ambition effectively damaged Democrats’ hopes in the 2024 presidential election.
“I was a little surprised she wrote that,” Jill Biden told MSNOW’s Morning Joe, adding that “Joe and Kamala, me, Doug (Emhoff), I thought we were a great team.” She added that “when Joe got out, he handed over the reins to Kamala” and “had full confidence in her.”
Jill Biden made the revelation as part of her media tour promoting her new memoir about the Biden family's years in the White House.
The former first lady said her husband and Harris remain on good terms, noting Harris had called two days prior to check on Joe Biden's health.

Matthew Perry: Five fascinating things up for sale at the late Friends star’s auction
Fans will have the chance to own a piece of Perry’s legacy through a charity auction, which will be held on Friday 5 June
- Lot 14044: Letter from Jennifer Aniston - An emotional and revealing item, this handwritten letter from co-star Jennifer Aniston reads: ““Oh my… How am I going to live without your light in my life on a daily basis?”
- Lot 14094: The Velveteen Rabbit - Perry’s copy of of Morgery Williams’ The Velveteen Rabbit nods to season four of Friends, in which Chandler buys a first edition of the book for Kathy, his crush, and Joey’s girlfriend, on her birthday.
- Multiple lots: Friends scripts - There is plenty of Friends memorabilia up for grabs in the auction, including a script of the show’s final episode, signed by Perry and his five co-stars.
- Multiple lots: Batman memorabilia - Perry was vocal about his fixation with the caped crusader, and in the days leading up to his death, the actor shared several social media posts signing off as “Mattman”.
- Lot 1405: The West Wing Tapes - Perry, who had been a longtime fan of the series, played lawyer Joe Quincy in just three episodes of Aaron Sorkin’s award-winning political drama.
