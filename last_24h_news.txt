13 best lightweight and compact strollers, reviewed by a parenting writer
Compact buggies from the likes of Joolz and Bugaboo could be the only wheels you need
- Bookmark
Whether you’re holidaying with baby in tow, looking for something light and easy for the nursery run, or ready to downsize from a bulky travel system, a lightweight stroller can be a real game-changer for modern family life. With their slim frames, clever compact folds and nimble wheels, they are designed to make everyday outings feel easier. From hopping on public transport and navigating busy pavements to storing neatly in small homes or even fitting into aircraft overhead lockers, what are the key ingredients you should look for when hunting for the best travel stroller for your family?
Today’s compact strollers are far more versatile than they once were, with many suitable from birth thanks to a fully lie-flat seat, a compatible car seat, or becoming more readily available in 2026, as an attachable carrycot. In those early months, babies need to lie as flat as possible to support healthy breathing, spine development and hip positioning, so look for a recline of around 170–180 degrees if newborn suitability is important to you.
As your baby grows, your travel needs will likely change too. For urban adventures, a well-designed compact stroller with a comfortable seat, simple fold, good recline and reliable suspension can tick all the right boxes.
Finally, if you’re hoping to take your stroller on board as cabin luggage, always check directly with your airline first. Cabin approval is decided by the carrier, not the stroller brand, so it’s best to confirm before you travel.

Your questions about lightweight strollers answered
What is the best lightweight stroller?
Having clocked up the miles across a large selection of travel strollers, we’re yet to find one to surpass the Joolz aer2. It’s a fabulous buggy for most everyday adventures, yet fully geared for travelling with a baby or toddler. Yes, it’s expensive, but if you need a new buggy right from the birth of your child, for the amount of use you’ll get out of it, in our opinion it’s a justified price.
If your budget is a little smaller but you want a stroller where you can attach a car seat, check in as cabin luggage, and fold in a flash, the Cybex orfeo, ticks a lot of boxes and is more than £100 cheaper, but bear in mind accessories are sold separately.
How I tested lightweight strollers
After pounding the pavements last year, we’re back for a 2026 update and over the summer months we strolled in towns, cities and on paths. We assembled, folded and unfolded, and strapped our toddler into a selection of lightweight buggies.
