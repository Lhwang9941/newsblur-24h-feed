PPE fraudster shows off his £126k Porsche before police raid
- Jogesh Bhandari and Craig Morris ran a PPE scam during the pandemic, falsely claiming they could supply millions of nitrile gloves to businesses and healthcare providers.
- The fraudsters used the proceeds from their scheme to fund a lavish lifestyle, acquiring luxury cars, watches, jewellery, and undertaking worldwide travel.
- Footage showed Bhandari posing with a new £126,000 Porsche shortly before his arrest.
- Bhandari's wife, Meenakashi, was also convicted of money laundering, having used some of the defrauded money to repay her personal debts.
- All three defendants were arrested in February 2023, convicted on 15 July, and are scheduled to be sentenced on 21 August for fraud by false representation and money laundering.

Moment dog sparks deadly house fire that killed three pets
- A house fire in Maryland was accidentally started by a dog named Bo, who turned on a kitchen toaster.
- Ring camera footage showed Bo jumping onto the counter and inadvertently activating the toaster, which ignited nearby items.
- The homeowners were not at the property during the incident on 10 July.
- While Bo and another dog, Addie, were rescued by neighbours, a third dog, Dakota, and two pet cats tragically died in the blaze.
- Thirty firefighters brought the fire under control in 20 minutes, but it caused up to $150,000 worth of damage.
