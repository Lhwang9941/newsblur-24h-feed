How bison are transforming woodlands and bringing wildlife back
- A herd of European bison introduced to Blean Woods, Kent, are having a significant positive impact on woodland habitat management and regeneration.
- The project, led by Kent Wildlife Trust and Wildwood Kent, aims to restore complex habitats, enhance nature's resilience to climate change, and store carbon.
- The bison's natural behaviours, such as grazing and felling trees, open up the canopy, allowing woodland regeneration and creating new spaces for diverse wildlife.
- New “bison bridges” are expanding the herd's roaming area to 200 hectares, with two already completed and two more planned by year-end.
- The initiative is one of hundreds supported by £31 million raised by Postcode Lottery players over 20 years, which also funded the bison project's initial setup.

Harry Redknapp’s prank nearly made two stars quit I’m A Celebrity
- Football manager Harry Redknapp orchestrated a prank on I’m A Celebrity… South Africa, which nearly led to singer Sinitta and TV personality Gemma Collins abandoning the show.
- Sinitta recounted how Redknapp pretended to read details of a gruelling new trial, causing Collins to declare, “I’m a celebrity get me out of here,” with Sinitta also considering leaving.
- The prank involved Redknapp describing a fictional “House Of Hell” trial with rats and being tied up, which came after a physically exhausting day for the campmates.
- Former boxer David Haye, a fellow contestant, praised Collins for her resilience in the new series, noting she tackled “real heavy challenges” despite his initial low expectations based on her previous short stint in 2014.
- The pre-recorded all-star series, hosted by Ant and Dec, is set to begin on April 6 at 9pm on ITV1, with the public voting for the winner in a live grand final.
