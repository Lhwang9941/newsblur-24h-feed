Add as a favourite source on Google Add DutchNews as a favourite source on GoogleUp to 200 residents of a street in The Hague were allowed back into their homes at 11pm on Saturday, after police finished dismantling a drugs lab found in a house in the Segbroek district.
Residents of dozens of homes on the Meidoornstraat, along with their pets, were told to leave by 1pm on Saturday and were taken in at the district office on Fahrenheitstraat while the police and and a specialist firm removed the remaining chemicals.
The city council said on Sunday there was no longer any acute danger, and that the evacuation had been a precaution against exposure while the material was being taken away. Residents have been advised to keep their windows shut because of the smell.
Police were called to the house at around 1pm on Friday after a report of a strange smell, and found a lab still in operation. Officers shut down the production process by pumping out the chemicals. A 39-year-old man from The Hague who had come into contact with the liquids was taken to hospital and arrested.
A 34-year-old man from Voorburg was arrested at around 10pm on Friday. A 44-year-old from The Hague and a 71-year-old from Leiden were also held. Police are still investigating what type of lab it was and are canvassing the neighbourhood for witnesses and camera footage.
According to NOS, barrels holding thousands of litres of chemicals were found at the property, of which 500 to 750 litres were removed on Friday. A spokesman for the regional safety board told the broadcaster that a lab of that size would not normally be found in a residential area.

Malaysia aims for 600,000 South Korean arrivals in new travel drive
Last year, about 558,000 South Koreans visited Malaysia, making it the country’s eighth-largest source market for international visitors
Last year, about 558,000 South Koreans visited Malaysia, making it the country’s eighth-largest source market for international visitors. This year, Malaysia hopes to increase that figure to 600,000.
“Our arrivals from South Korea have yet to return to pre-pandemic levels,” Kamilia said. “Before Covid-19, we welcomed more than 600,000 South Korean visitors, so this is an important milestone for us.”
The tourism drive forms part of the government’s global Visit Malaysia 2026 campaign, which aims to attract 47 million international visitors despite growing uncertainties affecting the global travel industry.
At the heart of the campaign is the theme “Surreal Experiences” – reflecting a shift in travellers’ expectations following the pandemic.

US President Donald Trump praised the Pittsburgh-based grocery chain Giant Eagle on Sunday after it announced that it would lower prices on more than 300 products through Labor Day to help American families.
Trump said the move demonstrates the retailer's commitment to reducing costs for consumers and compared the effort to similar pricing initiatives by Walmart.
"I have just been informed that Giant Eagle, a GREAT American Grocery Company, will be lowering Prices, by a lot, across more than 300 products this Summer, through Labor Day, to help hardworking American families.
"Giant Eagle, like Walmart, is stepping up in a big and bold way to answer my call to lower costs for working families. We will continue to bring Prices DOWN, just like Oil, Gas, Eggs, and Prescription Drugs, all of which are dropping FAST after the disaster we inherited from Sleepy Joe Biden," he said on his Truth Social account.
He called on other grocery chains to follow suit by cutting prices.
Trump also said his administration has helped drive down the cost of essentials including gasoline, eggs and prescription drugs while criticizing the previous Biden administration over inflation and rising living costs.
