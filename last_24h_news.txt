Follow live: Artemis II's final lunar flyby
Artemis II set a new distance record on Monday, breaking the previous record set by the Apollo 13 mission, for the farthest spaceflight from Earth. During the lunar flyby, which will last for several hours, the Artemis II crew will study specific lunar sites and phenomena as part of a 10-goal plan by NASA.
The four astronauts embarking on NASA's lunar flyby became on Monday the humans to travel furthest from our planet, as they get set to view areas of the Moon never before seen by the naked eye.

The Artemis II team broke the previous record set by 1970's Apollo 13 mission, which they are expected to surpass by approximately 4,105 miles (6,606 kilometres) when they reach this journey's anticipated furthest distance from Earth – 252,760 miles (406,778 kilometres) – later today.
