Can Hong Kong’s upgraded Shenzhen crossing really cut clearance to just 5 minutes?
New Huanggang crossing will feature joint clearance with 134 automated channels, cutting immigration process by 25 minutes, authorities say
An upgraded border crossing between Hong Kong and Shenzhen is expected to open next month, allowing travellers to complete immigration in just five minutes, according to authorities.
The redeveloped Huanggang Port Building will be the first link between the two cities to adopt the “collaborative inspection and joint clearance” model.
The South China Morning Post looks at the details of the checkpoint and what travellers can expect from the streamlined process.
1. How does it differ from the Lok Ma Chau/Huanggang crossing?
The new Huanggang complex will replace the existing Lok Ma Chau/Huanggang crossing as the only 24-hour link between Hong Kong and Shenzhen.
Commissioned in 1989, the original crossing requires passengers to clear Hong Kong immigration, take a shuttle bus across the Shenzhen River, and complete clearance on the other side.
The new complex, expected to open in July according to Chinese state media, will eliminate the need for shuttle buses, with immigration facilities from both sides housed under one roof.

Reuters: Ukrainian drone attack forces Kuibyshev oil refinery in Russia to halt operations
Ukraine’s drone attack on Samara on June 10 forced the Kuibyshev oil refinery to halt crude processing, two industry sources told Reuters.
The attack shut down two primary processing units at the refinery, both damaged in the strike and subsequent fires. Each unit processes about 10,000 tons, or 73,000 barrels, per day.
The refinery has made no official announcement of the shutdown.
Three people were injured in the drone attack in Samara region, Samara Governor Vyacheslav Fedorishchev said. He said several industrial facilities were also damaged but did not identify them. The independent Telegram channel Astra reported a fire at the Kuibyshev refinery after reviewing photos and videos from eyewitnesses.
The Kuibyshev oil refinery is one of the leading refineries in the Volga region, specializing in the production of gasoline and diesel fuel to the highest environmental standard. The refinery, along with the Novokuibyshev and Syzran refineries, is part of Rosneft’s Samara hub.
The Novokuibyshev refinery, according to Reuters, has been mostly offline since mid-April, and the Syzran refinery has been fully offline since mid-May. They were also targeted by Ukrainian drones.
Due to Ukrainian military attacks on oil facilities in Russia, a fuel crisis has begun. By June 10, shortages were reported in at least 25 regions.

Iran’s ‘unity of theatres’ strategy exposes US-Israel rift
Analysts say Iran’s new tactic is both an attempt to reassert strategic deterrence and a political bargaining chip ahead of negotiations
The rapid escalation in hostilities between Iran and the US-Israel alliance since Sunday is being driven by Tehran’s growing confidence that it can arise like a mythical phoenix from the ashes of war, strategically stronger than ever in the Middle East.
To achieve this objective, Tehran has enacted a tactical shift in its “unity of the theatres” strategy involving its Axis of Resistance allies in Lebanon, Yemen and Iraq: Israeli and US attempts to decapitate one head of this hydra-like monster, Hezbollah, would incur the wrath of the other heads, Iran and the Houthis.
So when Israel bombed a Hezbollah command centre in the crowded southern Dahiyeh suburbs of Beirut on Sunday, Iran and the Houthis retaliated by carrying out limited, largely symbolic missile drone attacks against Israel.
This unprecedented tactical response surprised Israel and the US – and exposed tensions between President Donald Trump and Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu over who “calls the shots” in the war.
While obviously insufficient in scope to deter subsequent escalations by the US against Iran, and by Israel against Hezbollah in southern Lebanon, Tehran nonetheless pushed Trump into leaning on Netanyahu to wrap up Israel’s retaliatory air strikes in 24 hours.
The Islamic Republic celebrated this as the foiling of Israeli and US efforts to decouple the conflict with Hezbollah from negotiations to end the Iran war.
