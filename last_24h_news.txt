Iran's Araghchi to travel to Oman on Saturday for talks on Hormuz Strait, IRNA says
Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi will travel to Oman on Saturday as the head of a diplomatic delegation for talks on bilateral relations and regional developments, particularly the situation in the Strait of Hormuz, Iran's official news agency IRNA reported on Friday.
AOC calls to release Gaza hospital Dr. Hussam Abu Safiya
U.S. Representative Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez called on Israel to "release pediatrician Dr. Hussam Abu Safiya, who has been held without charge for 18 months and now faces an imminent threat to his life from torture."
She added that Israel must "end the targeting of health workers and the inhumane treatment of Palestinians in arbitrary detention."
AOC's comments come after top U.S. Democratic lawmakers urged Israeli authorities to release the Gaza hospital director, who has been held without charge since his arrest in late 2024 in "life-threatening danger," according to his lawyer. They are calling on the United States to halt aid to Israel so long as Israel does not facilitate his release.
U.S. Treasury Department sanctions top Iranian financier
The U.S. Treasury Department issued sanctions against Dubai-based Iranian financial facilitator Ali Ansari, who they say "oversees a sprawling global network of assets benefitting Iran's leader" and "other regime elites."
"Ansari has effectively institutionalized large‑scale embezzlement within the Iranian regime, diverting publicly funded wealth into an extensive overseas portfolio of real estate and commercial holdings to enrich himself, regime elites," and "the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC)," the Office of Foreign Assets Control said.
OFAC said they targeted key Iranian exchange houses that "move billions of dollars annually on behalf of sanctioned Iranian banks, using layers of shell companies to obscure the regime's illicit financial activity."
"Treasury will continue using every tool at its disposal to isolate him and other regime elites from the global financial system," said Secretary of the Treasury Scott Bessent.
Fire breaks out at mini-refinery in western Iran, Tasnim says
A fire broke out on Friday at the Oxin Palayesh mini-refinery in Iran's western Lorestan province, the semi-official Tasnim news agency reported.
The fire began shortly after 5 p.m. local time and sent a column of thick smoke over part of Poldokhtar, Tasnim said.
Firefighters, rescue teams and heavy and light equipment were sent to the site, but the scale of the blaze and the presence of flammable materials made containment difficult and prevented crews from approaching the main seat of the fire in the initial hours, Tasnim added.
The semi-official Mehr news agency earlier cited Lorestan deputy governor as saying no deaths or injuries had been reported and that the cause of the fire was under investigation.
Preliminary reports indicated the fire had likely started in the raw materials section of the industrial unit and that possible negligence was being considered, though the cause could not be determined conclusively until the fire was extinguished, the deputy governor added.
German parliament backs bill that criminalizes denial of Israel's right to exist
Germany's Bundesrat, the upper house of parliament, backed a bill to criminalize the denial of Israel's right to exist on Friday, a motion that constitutional experts said could jeopardize freedom of expression.
According to the bill, anyone denying Israel's right to exist or calling for its abolition would be punished with a prison sentence of up to five years under the regulation. The bill will be examined by the Bundestag, the lower house of parliament, after its summer recess.
The offence would only apply if it could "encourage a willingness to commit anti-Semitic acts of violence or arbitrary measures."
Pakistan's Sharif speaks to Iranian president, reaffirms Islamabad's role as mediator
Pakistan's Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif said on Friday he had spoken with Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian on the ongoing war in the Middle East and reaffirmed Islamabad's readiness to continue its role as a mediator.
"We discussed the evolving regional situation and underscored the imperative of restraint, dialogue and diplomacy to safeguard the hard-earned peace gains of recent months," Sharif said in an X post.

Half of Jewish Americans don't think protesting Israel is antisemitic, poll finds
Jewish Americans are sharply split on what counts as antisemitism when it comes to criticism of Israel, a AP-NORC poll published Friday has found.
Jewish adults are divided over whether protesting an event related to Israel is an act of prejudice against Jewish people generally. About half of American Jews say anti-Israel protests are not a form of antisemitism, but roughly 4 in 10 say they are.
The war in Gaza sparked U.S. protests over Israel's military actions and coincided with an increase in violent attacks against U.S. Jewish communities. Protests surrounding speakers tied to Israel – whether Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's address to Congress or college speakers seen as too supportive of the country – became more common following the backlash over Israel's war.
