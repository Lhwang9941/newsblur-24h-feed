Earlier, two US officials confirmed the rescue to Reuters late on Saturday.

Israel Police caught a murder suspect on March 25 who had been hiding in an IDF military base.
The murder victim was a 32-year-old resident of Rehovot, who had been stabbed one morning in February outside of a club in Tel Aviv.
Police arrived at the scene and launched an immediate investigation. According to the police, the pursuit involved multiple units, including the Ayalon District Crime Fighting Unit, working in tandem to catch the perpetrator.
The suspected murderer, a 21-year-old resident of Rehovot, was eventually located and caught in an army base, where he was arrested.
Police arrest three accused of murders in Arab community
The police arrested three suspects accused of murders in the Arab community, the police announced on March 30.
Two of the suspects were arrested due to their involvement in a case from earlier that week, in which a man in his 50s was shot in Tel Aviv.
The third suspect, an 18-year-old from Lod, is accused of a murder that took place on February 12, when a Lod resident in his 60s was also shot to death.

WASHINGTON - The United States has rescued an airman whose F-15 jet was downed in Iran, two US officials told Reuters late on April 4.
The officials did not immediately provide further details on the rescue.
The Pentagon did not immediately respond to a request for comment.
The airman was the second member of a two-person crew of an F-15 that Iran said on April 3 was brought down by its air defences.
Reuters reported on April 3 that the first member of the crew had been recovered. REUTERS
