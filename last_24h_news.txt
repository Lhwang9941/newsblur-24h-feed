Right-wing candidate Abelardo De La Espriella won the Colombian presidential elections in a tight race on Sunday.
De La Espriella, 47, has proposed a tough crackdown on crime, as well as the end to peace talks with armed groups and a boost to Colombia's oil and gas sector.
Cepeda, 63, has vowed to continue the policies of President Gustavo Petro, a former rebel and the country's first leftist president, which include state pension payments for the poor, union-backed labor reforms, peace talks with armed groups that have fought the state for decades, and a moratorium on new oil projects.
De La Espriella blames Petro for the country's economic and security woes and has vowed to lower taxes and reduce the size of the state by up to 40%, but has said he will preserve Petro's 23% increase in the minimum wage, along with other popular social measures.

Oh, brother! Brady Tkachuk getting traded to Florida to join Matthew Tkachuk, AP source says
Oh, brother! Brady Tkachuk getting traded to Florida to join Matthew Tkachuk, AP source says
Matthew Tkachuk and Brady Tkachuk are about to team up in Florida, after the Panthers pulled off another summertime blockbuster.
The Panthers and Ottawa were finalizing a deal where Florida would send four draft picks to the Senators for Brady Tkachuk, a person familiar with the terms said Sunday. The person spoke to The Associated Press on condition of anonymity because neither team had released details publicly.
The picks were Florida’s first-rounder this year, No. 9 overall, along with two picks acquired earlier Sunday in the Panthers’ move to send Mackie Samoskevich to Seattle, and another first-rounder in 2029.
Brady Tkachuk has spent the entirety of his eight-year NHL career in Ottawa, and now he joins his brother, Olympic teammate and podcast co-host in Florida — the place where Matthew Tkachuk has spent the last four seasons, winning two Stanley Cups and getting to the final three times.
The Athletic was first to report the pending trade.
Brady Tkachuk has two seasons left on the seven-year, $57.56 million contract he signed in October 2021 when he also became Ottawa’s captain. As recently as late April, he bristled at conversation about his name being in trade rumors following a first-round playoff exit.
It’s a move that makes sense for so many reasons and has seemed almost inevitable.
Matthew Tkachuk and Brady Tkachuk reveled in the chance to play alongside one another for USA Hockey at the 4 Nations Face-Off in 2025 (winning silver) and the Milan Cortina Olympics this year (winning gold, the first for the American men’s program on the Olympic stage since the miracle in Lake Placid 46 years ago).
Their parents — their dad is NHL legend Keith Tkachuk — have a home in Florida and are regular attendees at Panthers games, and the USA Hockey ties means Brady Tkachuk already has relationships with many in the Florida organization. Bill Zito, Florida’s hockey operations president and general manager, had a role within USA Hockey during this past Olympic cycle and Panthers equipment manager Teddy Richards had a role with the 4 Nations and Milan Cortina teams as well.
Brady Tkachuk was Ottawa’s captain for the last five seasons, and he was — by far in some cases — their leader in countless categories over his eight years with the Senators. No player had more goals (213), points (463), power-play goals (62), shots (2,202), hits (1,921), winning goals (28), multigoal games (30) and penalty minutes (821) in his tenure with the club, which acquired him with the No. 4 pick in the 2018 draft.
And now, four years after landing Matthew Tkachuk, the Panthers have tapped into the Tkachuk family well of talent again.
It also adds another big name to the list of players that Florida has locked up for several seasons going forward, including Aleksander Barkov, Aaron Ekblad, Sam Reinhart, Sam Bennett, Carter Verhaeghe, Gustav Forsling, Anton Lundell and others. They’re all Stanley Cup winners, and now Brady Tkachuk would figure to have a real chance to get his name etched onto hockey’s chalice in the coming years as well.

The two scenarios facing No 10 if Starmer resigns as prime minister
Sir Keir Starmer faces crunch day on Monday after a weekend reflecting on his political future
- Bookmark
- CommentsGo to comments
Sir Keir Starmer is weighing up whether he can stay on as prime minister with a growing expectation amongst allies that he could announce a timetable for departure on Monday.
Sir Keir has spent the weekend at his grace-and-favour residence Chequers with his wife Victoria and his closest allies considering his political future.
A number of cabinet ministers told him frankly on Friday that he needed to set out a clear plan to step down, but Downing Street was insistent that Sir Keir would continue to fight on.
Monday will see Andy Burnham return to Westminster fresh from his victory in the Makerfield by-election. Labour MPs have been invited to a photo call with the former Greater Manchester mayor to celebrate Mr Burnham’s resounding victory over Reform UK.
Mr Burnham is reportedly keen to wait to become prime minister in September after summer recess rather than be crowned imminently. A delayed handover would allow Sir Keir Starmer “to secure his legacy”, one source told The Independent.
But some Labour MPs are anxious that the transfer of power takes place sooner, and have suggested as early as mid-July.
So what are the options ahead if Sir Keir announces his resignation on Monday?
An Andy Burnham coronation
If Sir Keir announced his resignation, a Labour leadership contest would be triggered with Mr Burnham needing the backing of 20 per cent of the party’s MPs. There are 403 Labour MPs, so the support of 81 would be needed.
A contest could also be launched even if Sir Keir did not resign, so long as Mr Burnham got the required support.
Once triggered, other candidates could join if they also had 81 backers.
But if Sir Keir decided not to stand, and no other Labour candidates reached 81 backers, then Mr Burnham would be the only candidate and would become prime minister.
The Burnham camp are reportedly keen that there is a planned transition between Sir Keir and the MP for Makerfield, allowing time for Mr Burnham to prepare for office.
If Sir Keir announces he will step down in September, this could give more time for a more orderly hand over. Chief secretary to the prime minister Darren Jones has reportedly already met with Louise Haigh, the former transport secretary who is a key Burnham supporter.
But some Labour MPs are keen that - if the keys to No 10 are passed to Mr Burnham - it happens quickly to avoid a public, potentially damaging, debate about the future of the Labour party.
An open contest for party leadership
In an alternative scenario, the former health secretary Wes Streeting or another challenger could get enough nominations to go up against Mr Burnham in a leadership challenge.
If the candidates get enough MP support, and also nominations from at least five per cent of all local party branches or at least three party-affiliated groups, then there would be a vote of Labour members.
The timing of this vote would be set by the party’s national executive committee. Party members and affiliated trade union supporters vote by ranking the candidates in order of preference.
If one candidate received more than 50 per cent of the first preferences then they win the contest. If this doesn’t happen, then the worst performing candidate gets eliminated. Votes are then transferred from this candidate to the remaining options until someone gets more than half of the votes.
On Friday, Sir Keir appeared to be talking to cabinet ministers about what arguments he would make in a leadership contest, telling them he would fight to stay as prime minister. However the mood music changed over the weekend, with allies of the prime minister believing he is now resigned to the fact he will have to step down.
