A French UNIFIL soldier was killed, and three others were wounded during an attack in southern Lebanon, French President Emmanuel Macron announced in an X/Twitter post on Saturday.
"Everything suggests that responsibility for this attack lies with Hezbollah," Macron wrote. "France demands that the Lebanese authorities immediately arrest the perpetrators and take their responsibilities alongside UNIFIL."
Macron identified the soldier as Sgt. First Class Florian Montorio of the 17th Parachute Engineer Regiment from Montauban.
Three other UNIFIL soldiers were wounded in the attack, and all of them have been evacuated to a nearby hospital.
Lebanese Prime Minister condemns attack
Lebanon's Prime Minister Nawaf Salam condemned the attack in an X/Twitter post on Saturday, saying such attacks cause "great harm to Lebanon."
I condemn the assault today on elements of the French battalion in UNIFIL," said Salam. "I have issued my strict instructions to conduct an immediate investigation to uncover the circumstances of this assault and to hold the perpetrators accountable."
This is a developing story.
