MOSCOW, May 11. /TASS/. Rescuers have located the tourists stranded on Mount Elbrus, they are in satisfactory condition, a representative of the Russian Emergencies Ministry told TASS.
"Russian Emergencies Ministry rescuers have found two tourists on Elbrus. According to preliminary information, the men are in satisfactory condition," the ministry official said.
Evacuation is currently underway.
It was earlier reported that on Sunday evening, two tourists became stranded on Elbrus at an altitude of 5,100 meters, unable to descend on their own. Rescuers from the Elbrus High-Mountain Search and Rescue Team were dispatched to assist them. The operation is hampered by freezing temperatures and a snowstorm. The tourists had not registered their route with the ministry.

British passengers from hantavirus-hit cruise ship return to UK for isolation
- Twenty British passengers, evacuated from the MV Hondius cruise ship due to a hantavirus outbreak, have been repatriated from Tenerife to the UK.
- The passengers arrived at Manchester Airport on Sunday evening via a chartered flight and were subsequently taken to Arrowe Park Hospital on the Wirral for initial isolation.
- They will undergo up to 72 hours of isolation at the managed facility, with public health specialists later assessing if they can complete a 45-day self-isolation period at home.
- Wirral University Teaching Hospital NHS Foundation Trust confirmed that no repatriated passengers are symptomatic, and the risk of hantavirus to the general public is considered "really low" due to its requirement for close contact.
- The World Health Organisation reported six confirmed hantavirus cases linked to the ship, with three British nationals among the eight total cases, two of whom are hospitalised abroad.
