All three of the nuclear-powered submarines Australia will receive under the AUKUS military deal will be second-hand, Defense Minister Richard Marles said on Sunday, touting the "significant" savings under the revised deal.
Under the original deal inked in 2021, Australia was expected to receive at least two used and one new Virginia-class submarine, public outlet the Australian Broadcasting Corporation reported.
Marles, however, told reporters that under the revised deal, Australia will now purchase only second-hand Virginia-class submarines from the US.
The joint US-UK-Australia statement described the change as a mutual streamlining "simplifying supply-chain management, operational and maintenance requirements, and maximising cost efficiencies."
The project under the AUKUS alliance, among the US, UK, and Australia, is seen as crucial to enhancing defense capabilities.
For the project, the UK had pledged £4 billion ($5.3 billion), the US $17.5 billion, and Australia more than 30 billion Australian dollars ($20.7 billion).

Man on the run after cross-town stabbings in Epping, Dandenong North
Police are searching for a man who allegedly stabbed two people across two suburbs and crashed a car into a Dandenong North home on Sunday.
Police allege the man, described as being of African appearance and wearing a brown shirt and black pants, stabbed an Epping man, whom he knew, at a house on Bradman Terrace about 7.30am. The victim, aged in his 40s, was taken to hospital in a stable condition with upper body injuries.
The man then travelled to Dandenong North, with police alleging he deliberately crashed a silver Honda CRV into an Exner Drive house about 10.15am, before exiting the vehicle and stabbing a woman in her 30s, whom he also knew.
He then fled the scene on foot, police say.
The woman was taken to hospital in a serious but stable condition with upper body injuries.
Police believe both incidents were targeted, as the man was known to both stabbing victims.
They have asked the public not to approach the man and call Triple Zero if they see him.
Police have also appealed for anyone with video, information or dashcam footage of these incidents to contact crimestoppersvic.com.au or call 1800 333 000.
